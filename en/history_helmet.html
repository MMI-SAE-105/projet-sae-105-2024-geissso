THE IMPORTANCE OF HELMETS IN FORMULA 1 TODAY

The helmets worn by Formula 1 drivers are crucial for their safety, as they provide protection against high-speed impacts, extreme shocks, and heavy objects. Designed to withstand a weight of 10 kg dropped from a height of 5 meters and to resist the impact of a metal disc launched at 250 km/h, they also feature a visor capable of protecting against flying debris.

In the event of a fire, helmets must withstand temperatures reaching 790°C. Following accidents such as Felipe Massa's in 2009, safety standards have been strengthened, leading to a constant evolution of materials used, such as carbon fiber, which allows for the creation of lighter and more resistant helmets.

Moreover, these helmets protect not only the skull but also the driver's face and eyes, while working in synergy with other safety systems such as the HANS, thus ensuring optimal protection during high-speed races.
