THE COMPOSITION OF A FORMULA 1 HELMET

The Visor

The visor of a Formula 1 helmet is a crucial element for driver safety, as it protects not only the eyes but also the face from flying debris, impacts, and weather conditions. Designed to withstand rigorous testing, the visor must endure impacts such as those from an air rifle shot and support extreme temperatures up to 790°C. This protection is particularly important in a sport where speeds often exceed 200 km/h.

The Carbon and Kevlar Structure

It promotes better temperature regulation and provides enhanced fire protection. Integrated with other safety systems like the HANS, the carbon and Kevlar structure is crucial for ensuring optimal protection while remaining lightweight, which is essential in a sport where every gram matters for performance.

The Protection Zones

The extended lateral protection zones on a Formula 1 driver’s helmet play a crucial role in driver safety by providing optimal compatibility with the headrests of modern single-seaters and the lateral protection systems integrated into the seats. These zones ensure better energy management during side impacts, offering increased protection in situations where the HANS system is less effective. By limiting the lateral movements of the driver's head, they help prevent severe cervical injuries by reducing abrupt movements during an impact.

The HANS

The HANS (Head And Neck Support) system is an essential safety device in Formula 1, made mandatory since 2003, and plays a crucial role in protecting drivers during accidents, particularly in the event of frontal collisions. It reduces neck stress by approximately 80%, significantly limiting the risk of severe cervical injuries and preventing whiplash, which can cause irreparable damage to the spine and brain. By restricting head movement while allowing sufficient mobility to drive, the HANS helps reduce the severity of traumas, sprains, and fractures. Its effectiveness is proven, as it halves head displacement during a frontal impact. Designed in carbon fiber to be lightweight, weighing between 500 and 650 grams, it integrates perfectly with the helmet and safety harnesses, providing optimal protection.
